THERMAL AND STRUCTURAL ANALYSIS

Highlights from key analysis projects

Thermomechanical Analysis

I have worked extensively on thermomechanical analyses of cylinder heads and blocks. The combustion face for cylinder heads has to be designed such that it is capable of sustaining the high temperatures that it is subjected to. If the temperatures exceed a certain threshold, cracks start developing in the combustion face that can cause serious warranty issues. I have worked on designing and analyzing diesel, natural gas, and fuel cell engines to meet the temperature requirements. I am proficient at analyzing steady-state and transient thermal conditions in Ansys Workbench. I piloted the transient thermal analysis process for cylinder heads and gaskets using combustion CFD and coolant CFD domains. I regularly collaborate with CFD analysts who work on Converge CFD and Ansys Fluent. I delivered a 35 deg. C reduction in combustion face temperatures for a heavy-duty diesel engine as compared to the baseline product by modifying the water jacket design to target hot areas and by optimizing the coolant flow.

Modal and Stress Analysis

As part of my current role, I have to regularly analyze different components for stress and deformation under specific loading conditions. The load application requirements for different components vary based on their specific duty cycle and application. For instance, for a cylinder head or a block, the worst-case loads come from the combustion firing loads and thermal stresses. On the other hand, a component like a flywheel housing is more likely to fail from transportation loads. For a stationary energy unit like a Solid Oxide Fuel Cell power generator, some of the critical loads come from seismic and wind loads. I regularly work on various engines and components to formulate different loading and boundary conditions that make physical sense. Since I work on NPI programs, more often than not, the analysis procedures and pass/fail criteria have to be curated to reduce the ambiguity of the results.
